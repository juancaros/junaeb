Easing the burden: tracking and boosting children's health and socio-emotional skills

Dashboard

Forecasting human development for policy can be quite challenging. Fortunately, with data at the population level allows to understand more about the underpinnings that can drive child skills within school communities. Once data is processed (see Data section), we can construct and aggregate individual measures at the school level in order to provide insights to stakeholders. This platform can be tailored and used for program allocation, feedback to schools and parents, but also to provide customized messages in order to facilitate the promotion of child development at the national, local and individual level.

This prototype uses simulated data to how, using simple visualization, we can create a dashboard that can allow policymakers and other agents (e.g. parents) to understand the variation in child development across and within schools, as well as over time. In the panel, solid lines indicate population averages (standarized z-scores), while solid lines represent school averages. As an example, we show the following variables: average body mass index (BAZ), height-for-age (HAZ), socioemotional skills (Externalizing Behavior), and parental time investment.
